Mercari Usability Evaluation

Heuristic and user-based evaluation of Mercari's mobile marketplace, surfacing 14 issues including a severity-4 critical bug.

My Role
UX Researcher & Evaluator
Timeline
4 weeks
Tools
Figma, Notion, Zoom
Method
Heuristic eval + usability testing
🔍

The Problem

For a marketplace at Mercari's scale, small usability friction in core flows — listing, searching, checkout — translates into significant drop-off. I ran a structured evaluation using expert heuristic analysis and task-based usability testing to surface the highest-priority issues.

Evaluation Goals

  • How easily can a first-time seller list an item from start to publish?
  • How efficiently can a buyer search, filter, and evaluate an item?
  • Where does checkout create confusion or hesitation?

Method

Heuristic Evaluation

I evaluated the mobile app against Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics across the three primary flows, documenting violations with severity ratings on a 0–4 scale.

Usability Testing

5 participants with varying e-commerce experience, moderated think-aloud sessions over Zoom. Each completed 3 tasks: listing an item, finding and saving a product, completing a simulated purchase.

Key Findings

  • Severity 4 — Listing: After 6 photos, the app gives no indication the limit was reached. Users kept tapping Add Photo, assuming photos had failed.
  • Severity 3 — Search filters: Applying filters requires leaving the results page entirely. Users can't preview result counts while filtering.
  • Severity 3 — Checkout: Make Offer and Buy Now buttons are nearly identical in weight. 3 of 5 participants accidentally initiated an offer when they intended to buy.
  • Severity 2 — Condition labels: Terms like Good and Like New are undefined. Participants had inconsistent interpretations.

Recommendations

  • Add a real-time photo counter (X of 6 added) to the listing screen.
  • Inline filter previews updating result counts without leaving the results page.
  • Visually differentiate Make Offer and Buy Now — make Buy Now the clear primary CTA.
  • Add a condition guide via tooltip on item condition labels.

Results

Evaluation outcomes

  • 14 usability issues documented across 3 flows with severity ratings and annotated screenshots.
  • 5 of 5 participants encountered the photo limit bug, confirming severity-4 rating.
  • 3 of 5 participants clicked the wrong action (Make Offer vs. Buy Now) during checkout.
  • Delivered prioritized recommendations report with annotated Figma mockups for 4 proposed fixes.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

  • Heuristic evaluation is a starting point. The photo bug was something I caught in expert review, but 5 users hitting the same wall confirmed how universal it was. Both methods are necessary.
  • Visual hierarchy in CTAs is deceptively hard. Two buttons of equal weight placed side by side get tapped randomly. I now pay close attention to CTA hierarchy in everything I design.
  • Novice users are the most diagnostic. My most useful participant had never used Mercari before. Experienced users develop workarounds and stop noticing problems.
