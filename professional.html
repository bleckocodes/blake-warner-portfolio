Heroic Development - UX Designer (2024-2025)

Heroic Development builds board management software for organizational governance. I joined as a summer intern in 2023 and returned as a full designer during my senior capstone and after graduation. Over that time I contributed to the full core feature set of their SaaS platform.

What I worked on

  • Dashboard and navigation - information architecture and main navigation design across the core product
  • Meeting manager - end-to-end flow for scheduling, running, and documenting board meetings including agenda builder and minute-taking UI
  • Document editor - in-app document editing with real-time collaboration indicators and version history access
  • Member manager - admin interface for role assignment and organizational permission management
  • Login and authentication - sign-in, password reset, and first-time-setup flows
  • Responsive design - all features designed for both desktop and mobile-optimized layouts simultaneously

My process

Each feature began with a written workflow document - I mapped user flows and edge cases in writing before opening Figma. I then moved through wireframes, design review, high-fidelity mockups, and interactive prototypes for developer handoff. I participated in weekly design reviews and iterated based on developer feasibility feedback throughout the entire cycle.

Impact

  • Designed and delivered wireframes and prototypes for 6+ major feature areas from concept through developer handoff.
  • Conducted competitive analysis across 4 comparable SaaS platforms to benchmark usability and inform design decisions.
  • High-fidelity prototypes used directly in investor and stakeholder presentations, receiving positive feedback on quality and usability.

Supergreen - UX / Product Designer (2025-2026)

At Supergreen I worked across multiple concurrent SaaS products, contributing to a shared design system and individual product interfaces. The fast-paced environment demanded high-quality work delivered in tight weekly cycles.

What I worked on

  • Design system application - consistent component library applied across multiple products simultaneously
  • Light and dark mode - all interfaces designed for both modes, maintaining contrast ratios and visual hierarchy across themes
  • Messaging and notifications - real-time messaging UI, notification center, and alert patterns
  • Directory and resource features - searchable directory interfaces and resource library UIs for web and mobile
  • Feature specification - writing requirement documents before wireframing to align scope with product leads
  • Rapid iteration - operating in weekly review cycles, updating designs within tight turnaround windows

Impact

  • Contributed design deliverables across multiple SaaS products within a shared design system framework.
  • Maintained design-to-development handoff velocity within weekly sprint cycles.
  • Collaborated directly with developers to resolve implementation questions and preserve design quality through build.

Key Learnings

What working in industry taught me

  • Speed and quality require good systems, not a trade-off. Working in weekly review cycles made me better at scoping what could be designed well in a short window. Design systems were the key - a solid component library meant I could move fast without sacrificing consistency.
  • Writing before wireframing saved time, not lost it. Feature requirement documents forced scope clarity, surfaced edge cases early, and made developer conversations far more productive. I now do this on every project.
  • Feedback is the product. A design is not finished when you think it is good - it is finished when it has been tested by the team, reviewed by stakeholders, and validated against what developers can actually build. Learning to receive and act on feedback quickly without losing design quality is a real and learnable skill.
