Mercari Usability Evaluation

Heuristic and user-based evaluation of Mercari's mobile marketplace, surfacing 14 issues including a severity-4 critical bug.

My Role
UX Researcher and Evaluator
Timeline
4 weeks
Tools
Figma, Notion, Zoom
Method
Heuristic eval + usability testing
🔍

The Problem

For a marketplace at Mercari's scale, even small usability friction in core flows - listing, searching, and checkout - translates into significant drop-off at scale. I ran a structured evaluation using both expert heuristic analysis and task-based usability testing to surface the highest-priority issues.

Evaluation Goals

  • How easily can a first-time seller list an item from start to publish?
  • How efficiently can a buyer search, filter, and evaluate a specific item?
  • Where does the checkout and payment flow create confusion or hesitation?

Method

Heuristic Evaluation

I evaluated Mercari's mobile app against Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics, walking through the three primary flows and documenting violations with severity ratings on a 0 to 4 scale.

Usability Testing

I recruited 5 participants with varying e-commerce experience and conducted moderated think-aloud sessions over Zoom. Each participant completed 3 tasks: listing a used item for sale, finding and saving a specific product, and completing a simulated purchase.

Key Findings

  • Severity 4 - Listing flow: After 6 photos are uploaded, the app gives no indication the limit has been reached. Users kept tapping Add Photo with no feedback, assuming photos had failed to upload.
  • Severity 3 - Search filters: Applying multiple filters requires navigating to a separate screen. Users cannot preview result counts while filtering, making it impossible to evaluate choices before committing.
  • Severity 3 - Checkout: The Make Offer and Buy Now buttons are nearly identical in visual weight. 3 of 5 test participants accidentally initiated an offer when they intended to purchase directly.
  • Severity 2 - Condition labels: Terms like Good and Like New are undefined. Users in testing had inconsistent interpretations and expressed uncertainty about what they were actually buying.

Recommendations

  • Add a photo count indicator on the listing screen showing X of 6 photos added, updating in real time as photos are uploaded.
  • Implement inline filter previews that update result counts as filters are toggled, without requiring page navigation.
  • Visually differentiate Make Offer and Buy Now - make Buy Now the primary CTA and reduce the visual weight of Make Offer to a secondary action.
  • Add a standardized condition guide accessible via tooltip on item condition labels throughout the app.

Results

Evaluation outcomes

  • 14 usability issues documented across 3 core flows with severity ratings and annotated screenshots.
  • 5 of 5 usability test participants encountered the photo limit bug, confirming the severity-4 rating.
  • 3 of 5 participants clicked the wrong action button during checkout (Make Offer vs. Buy Now).
  • Final deliverable: A prioritized recommendations report with annotated Figma mockups for 4 proposed fixes.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

  • Heuristic evaluation is a starting point, not a conclusion. My severity-4 finding was something I caught in the expert review phase - but until 5 users hit the same wall in testing, I could not know how universal the problem was. Both methods are necessary, and they reinforce each other.
  • Visual hierarchy in CTAs is deceptively hard to get right. Two buttons of equal weight placed next to each other will get tapped in random order. I now pay close attention to CTA hierarchy and visual weight in every project I work on.
  • Recruiting novice users is essential. My most useful test participant had never used Mercari before. Their confusion was the most diagnostic. Experienced users develop workarounds and stop noticing the problems that matter most.
