Prayr App UX Research

Mixed-methods research for a faith-based mobile app, uncovering emotional and functional needs the product was not meeting.

My Role
UX Researcher (solo)
Timeline
5 weeks
Tools
Zoom, FigJam, Notion
Methods
Interviews, affinity map, personas
🙏

Project Overview

A focused UX research study for a faith-based mobile app. My goal was not to redesign the app but to deeply understand user mental models and pain points — and deliver findings that could directly inform the next design iteration.

Research Questions

  • How do people currently manage prayer practices, and what tools do they use?
  • What emotional and functional needs go unmet by existing tools?
  • Where does the app create friction, and what features resonate most?

Method

Participants

6 participants with active personal prayer practices who had used at least one digital tool for spiritual reflection. Ages 22 to 58, three Christian denominations.

Interviews

45-minute semi-structured interviews over Zoom. I focused on habits before introducing any questions about the app — understanding mental models before priming with product features.

Synthesis

Transcribed and coded all 6 interviews, clustered into an affinity diagram in FigJam. Identified 5 major themes and developed 2 primary user personas.

Key Findings

  • Prayer is deeply personal. Users were wary of anything that felt performative. The group prayer feature made several participants uncomfortable.
  • Physical journals were the primary competitor. 4 of 6 participants kept a physical journal. The non-screentime experience was part of the spiritual value.
  • Answered prayer tracking was the most valued and least developed feature. Users wanted to look back and see how prayers were answered over time.
  • Reminders were polarizing. Some loved daily prompts; others had turned them off immediately. A gentle, non-streak-based mode was consistently requested.
  • Simple is sacred. Users trusted the app more when it felt calm and uncluttered.

Design Recommendations

  • Private-by-default mode for all entries. Sharing should require explicit opt-in, not opt-out.
  • Elevate Answered Prayer in navigation — front-and-center, not buried in settings.
  • Flexible reminders with a gentle mode that nudges without streaks or pressure.
  • Simplified home screen showing only recent prayers and a single clear CTA.
  • Add a private reflection journal so users who journal don't need to switch apps.
  • Revise group sharing language to make privacy expectations explicit at the point of entry.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

  • Research on sensitive topics requires careful framing. My first two questions were too direct and produced surface-level answers. Restructuring to ask about habits before attitudes dramatically improved depth.
  • The most important insight came from something not in the app: physical journals. Understanding why users preferred analog helped me identify what the digital experience was failing to provide emotionally.
  • Personas built from real tension are more useful than averages. My two personas had genuinely conflicting needs. Holding both in mind made the recommendations stronger and more honest.
